# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Clinical radiology professionals and the people who report on their operations, using EcoRad in a browser during planning, reporting, or research, not in a time-critical clinical moment:

- **Radiologists and clinical leads** sizing their department's footprint and hunting quick wins.
- **Sustainability / ESG officers** producing Scope 1/2/3 estimates and intervention comparisons for reporting.
- **Medical physicists** benchmarking scanner energy against published literature.
- **AI governance teams** modelling the lifecycle carbon of a radiology AI tool before deployment.
- **Academic researchers** exploring and citing the evidence base and exporting data.
- **Healthcare executives** who need the story told in plain equivalencies (car km, flights, tree-years).

The job to be done: turn department parameters into a credible, citable environmental footprint and a short list of actions, fast enough to use in a meeting and rigorous enough to defend in a report.

## Product Purpose

A browser-based sustainability dashboard for clinical radiology departments. It quantifies energy, carbon (GHG Scopes 1/2/3), water, and AI-tool footprints, compares evidence-based interventions, and exports audit-ready reports. Every default is literature-sourced and overridable with measured data. Fully client-side: no backend, no data leaves the browser. Success is a user leaving with a defensible number and a clear next action they trust enough to cite.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, optimistic, green. Warm and encouraging about sustainability while staying evidence-led and honest. It should feel like a knowledgeable colleague who makes a complex footprint understandable, never preachy, never alarmist. Confidence comes from transparency (every number shows its source), not from decoration.

## Anti-references

Avoid all three of:

- **Generic AI/SaaS dashboard** — purple/indigo gradients, glassy cards, gradient text, the big-number hero-metric template, endless identical card grids.
- **Marketing / greenwash brochure** — vague eco-claims, stock nature imagery, feel-good fluff with no data behind it.
- **Cold, corporate, lifeless enterprise software** — gray, sterile, joyless.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the evidence.** Every number carries its source and confidence; credibility is the product, so never hide the citation.
2. **Practice what you preach.** A sustainability tool should itself feel calm, efficient, and uncluttered, not wasteful of the user's attention.
3. **One insight per view.** Surface the headline and the action first; reveal supporting detail progressively rather than dumping everything at once.
4. **Honest, not alarmist.** Encourage action with optimism and clarity; let the data make the case without scare tactics or greenwash.
5. **Measured beats assumed.** Make it obvious which inputs are measured vs estimated, and easy to override defaults with real data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, and readable text.
- **Color-blind safe is a priority:** charts and status must never rely on color alone. Use direct data labels, text, position, or pattern alongside any color encoding (the data viz is heavily green, so this matters).
- Respect reduced-motion preferences for any animation.
