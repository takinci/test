# Item 8 — Inclusion and exclusion criteria

### Specify inclusion and exclusion criteria, such as location, dates, patient-care setting, demographics (e.g., age, sex, race), pertinent follow-up, and results from prior tests. Define how, where, and when potentially eligible participants or studies were identified. Indicate whether a consecutive, random, or convenience series was selected.

Explanation and Elaboration: Understanding the composition of a dataset is key to evaluating the validity and transferability of machine learning models based on the dataset. In addition to explicit inclusion and exclusion criteria, which have obvious impact on dataset composition, any sampling procedure must be described as this can influence the composition. Sampling methods other than consecutive or fully randomized samples often introduce biases into a dataset.

| Mammography and pathology data records for 915,334 screening and diagnostic examinations from 94,656 patients were obtained from a single site at US Dataset 1, acquired from 2008 to 2017. From this dataset, digital breast tomosynthesis (DBT) examinations and those in patients with breast implants were removed. This process resulted in a total of 117,844 screening examinations from 38,456 patients with a mean age of 57.4 years (standard deviation 10.2). The examinations were acquired on two scanner models: Hologic Selenia (HSE) (39,710 examinations, 33.7%) and Hologic Selenia Dimensions (SED) (78,134 examinations, 66.3%). The examinations were interpreted by one of 11 radiologists with subspecialties in breast imaging and experience ranging from 2 to 30 years. Among the examinations, 979 (0.8%) were cancer-positive confirmed by biopsy, 78 (0.07%) were interval cancers, and 1,638 (1.4%) were benign confirmed by biopsy. In addition to the screening examinations, 25,749 diagnostic examinations available from the same data source were utilized to augment training. These diagnostic examinations were gathered such that all images had image-level annotations localizing any findings provided retrospectively by a radiologist with breast imaging subspecialty and magnification factors no larger than 1.1, therefore removing the magnified views. These diagnostic examinations added 2,555 examinations that were cancer positive confirmed by biopsy. \[Appendix S1, Data Acquisition and Labeling section [(23)](https://doi.org/10.1148/ryai.230033), CC-BY 4.0\] |
