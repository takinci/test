# Item 5 — Prospective or retrospective study

### Indicate if the study is prospective or retrospective. Evaluate AI models in a prospective setting, if possible. 

Explanation and Elaboration: Prospective evaluation is preferred because it evaluates the model in a setting more similar to the expected clinical use of the model. Retrospective evaluation is usually faster and less expensive, but more likely to be subject to confounding and bias. In complex multimodal clinical datasets it is often difficult to establish what data would have been available at the time the model would have run – for example a single note or report may include data obtained both before and after an imaging study was performed. Where it is not feasible to perform prospective evaluation, there should generally be discussion of potential biases and confounders due to retrospective evaluation in the discussion section.
