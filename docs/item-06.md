# Item 6 — Study goal

### Describe the study’s design and goals, such as model creation, exploratory study, feasibility study, or noninferiority trial. For classification systems, state the intended use, such as risk assessment, triage, diagnosis, screening, staging, monitoring, surveillance, prediction, or prognosis. Describe the type of predictive modeling to be performed, the target of predictions, and how it will solve the clinical or scientific question.

Explanation and Elaboration: The study design and goal is the bridge between the broader context of the study and its specific execution, ensuring clarity about what the research seeks to achieve and how the research will help accomplish the stated problem. For example, a classification model for triaging chest X-rays requires different design and testing considerations than a segmentation model for delineating tumors. A well-defined study design ensures transparency, guides appropriate methodological decisions, and establishes the criteria against which the research’s success will be evaluated.
