# Item 15 — Rationale for choosing the reference standard

### Describe the rationale for choice of the reference standard versus any alternatives. Include information on potential errors, biases, and limitations of that reference standard.

Explanation and Elaboration: A good reference standard should minimize error and ambiguity, and align with the study's objectives. For example, in segmentation tasks, if the task is straightforward then the reference standard is often manual segmentation by an expert. If the segmentation task is challenging, or the decisions which it informs require high precision, then multiple human experts may be warranted. Alternatively, in cases where both the imaging system and segmentation contribute to error, or the contribution of each is unknown, a more rigorous evaluation may be necessary. This may be the case when an imaging modality is being used to measure a new tissue of interest it is not validated for. In this case, the reference standard may require physical measurement methods, such as dissection and direct tissue volume measurement, to establish a more objective reference. This principle applies broadly across AI research: when the chosen reference standard may obscure the contributions of different error sources or lacks precision, alternative or supplementary methods should be employed.
