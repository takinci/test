# Item 28 — Metrics of model performance

### Describe the metrics used to assess the model’s performance and indicate how they address the performance characteristics most important to the clinical or scientific problem…. Compare the presented model to previously published models.

Explanation and Elaboration: There are a wide variety of performance metrics available for evaluating models, some common in data science and others more common in diagnostic medicine. Select metrics that focus on measuring the aspects of performance that are most relevant to the specific problem you are addressing. Typically, these are metrics commonly found in non-AI medical diagnostics manuscripts, such as sensitivity, specificity, and area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUC-ROC). Metrics that are dependent on prevalence, including positive and negative predictive values and overall accuracy, should be avoided unless prevalence in the test set is similar to the population to which the model will be applied. It may be appropriate to secondarily report non-medical metrics commonly used in AI to facilitate comparison with other models.
